Detection Steps for Fluorescein Diacetate (FDA)

(Lundgren, 1981)
  1. Preparation of the FDA stock solution

      2. Prepare the Fluorescein Diacetate (FDA) stock solution at 20 μmol/L concentration.

    1. Weigh 0.8328 mg of Fluorescein Diacetate (FDA) using an analytical balance.

    2. Transfer the 0.8328 mg of FDA powder into a 100 mL volumetric flask for the preparation of a 20 μmol/L stock solution. Gradually add acetone while gently swirling to dissolve the FDA completely. Continue adding acetone until the total volume reaches 100 mL.

    3. Swirl the flask gently to ensure the FDA is fully dissolved. Transfer the 20 μmol L-1 FDA stock solution to a sterile amber glass bottle. Seal the bottle with Parafilm and store the solution at -20°C. Before use, allow the solution to reach room temperature and mix gently.

  2. Preparation of the Sodium Phosphate buffer solution

    • Dissolve 22.74 g of sodium phosphate tribasic (Na₃PO₄·12H₂O) in 700 mL deionized water. Adjust pH to 7.6 with 1 M HCl. Dilute to a final volume of 1 L.

  3. Preparation of soil sample

    1. Weigh 1.0 g of soil and transfer it to a sterile beaker.

    2. Add 10 mL of sodium phosphate buffer to the beaker to hydrate the soil sample. Stir the mixture for 5 minutes to ensure even dispersion of particles.

  4. Reaction Phase

    • Mix 9 mL of the filtered soil sample with 0.5 mL of the FDA stock solution. Incubate at room temperature (27°C) for 10 minutes to allow reaction.
  5. Fluorescent Measurement

    1. Extract 1 mL of the solution and measure the fluorescence of the solution using a fluorescence spectrometer.

    2. Set the excitation wavelength to 490 nm and the emission wavelength to 514-520 nm to detect the enhanced fluorescence of FAD.

Reagent Required:

  1. Fluorescein Diacetate (FDA)
  2. Acetone
  3. Sodium phosphate tribasic (Na₃PO₄·12H₂O)
  4. HCl
