Detection Steps for Flavin adenine dinucleotide (FAD)

(Rhee et al, 2007)
  1. Preparation of the PTZ-DPA buffer solution

    1. Weigh out the 6.31 mg of bis(Zn²⁺-dipicolylamine) complex for a 1 mL of 10 mM solution.

    2. Dissolve the bis(Zn²⁺-dipicolylamine) complex in a 1 mL of the DMSO solution to ensure complete dissolution.

    3. Once fully dissolved, transfer the solution to a volumetric flask.

    4. Dilute the stock solution into the HEPES buffer (pH 7.4) with the ratio of PTZ-DPA:HEPES = 1:999). It can be 0.01 mL of PTZ-DPA mixed with 9.99 mL of HEPES.

    5. Mix thoroughly to ensure the solution is homogeneous.

    6. Store the 1 mM bis(Zn²⁺-dipicolylamine) complex solution in a sterile, tightly sealed container at -20 until use. Protect from light.

  2. Preparation of soil solution:

    • Dissolve 1g of the soil in 10 mL of the HEPES buffer solution.

  3. Reaction Phase

    • Mix 50 µL of the filtered soil sample with 10 µM PTZ-DPA buffer solution. Incubate at room temperature (27°C) for 10 minutes to allow FAD binding and reaction.

  4. Fluorescent Measurement

    1. Extract 1 mL of the solution and measure the fluorescence of the solution using a fluorescence spectrometer.

    2. Set the excitation wavelength to 445 nm and the emission wavelength to 525 nm to detect the enhanced fluorescence of FAD.

Reagent Required:

  1. bis(Zn²⁺-dipicolylamine) complex
  2. DMSO
  3. HEPES
  4. Flavin adenine dinucleotide
