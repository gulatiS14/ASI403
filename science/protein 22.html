Detection Steps for Protein

(Steinberg, 2009)
  1. Preparation of the Fluorescamine stock solution

    1. Prepare the Fluorescamine stock solution at 3 mg/ mL concentration in acetone.

    2. 1. Add 60 mg of Fluorescamine powder into 20 mL of acetone in a 50 mL volumetric flask for the preparation of a 3 mg/ mL acetone stock solution.
    3. 2. Swirl the flask gently to ensure the Fluorescamine is fully dissolved. Transfer the 3 mg/ mL acetone Fluorescamine stock solution to a sterile amber glass bottle. Seal the bottle with Parafilm and store the solution at -20Â°C. Before use, allow the solution to reach room temperature and mix gently.

  2. Preparation of soil sample

    • Dissolve 1g of the soil in 10 mL of the phosphate-buffered saline (PBS).

  3. Stirring Phase

    1. Stir it for 5 minutes. Filter out the soil.

    2. Reaction Phase

      1. Mix 5 mL of the filtrate with 0.2 mL of the Fluorescamine stock solution. Stir for another 5 minutes.
    3. Fluorescence Measurement

      1. Extract 3 mL of the solution and measure the fluorescence of the solution using a fluorescence spectrometer.
      2. Set the excitation wavelength to 365 nm to detect the fluorescence of Fluorescamine.

    Reagent Required:

    1. Fluorescamine
    2. Acetone
    3. Phosphate-Buffered Saline (PBS)
