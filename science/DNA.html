Detection Steps for nucleosides, nucleotides, and DNA-like molecules

(Azab et al, 2015)
  1. Fluorescent Probe Preparation

    1. Prepare a solution containing 1x10⁻⁵ M Europium complex, 2x10⁻⁵ M Cyclen, and 1x10⁻⁵ M TNB (4,4,4-trifluoro-1-(2-naphthyl)-1,3-butanedione).

  2. Buffer Solution Preparation

    • Prepare acetonitrile-isopropanol-0.1 M ammonium acetate-28% ammoniumhydroxide (60:10:20:10) solution.

  3. Sample Preparation

    1. Weigh 1.0 g of soil and transfer it to a sterile beaker.

    2. Add 10 mL of buffer solution to the beaker to hydrate the soil sample. Stir the mixture for 5 minutes to ensure even dispersion of particles.

  4. Fluorescent Probe Addition

    1. Add 0.1 mL of the fluorescent solution into 10 mL of filtered soil sample.

    2. Stir briefly to mix the solution evenly with the soil extraction. Leave it for 5 minutes.

  5. Fluorescence Detection

    1. Detect the fluorescence using a fluorescence spectrometer.

    2. Set the excitation wavelength to 340 nm and the emission wavelength to 614 nm to detect the fluorescence.

Reagent Required:

  1. Europium complex
  2. Cyclen
  3. TNB (4,4,4-trifluoro-1-(2-naphthyl)-1,3-butanedione)
  4. Acetonitrile
  5. Ammonium acetate
  6. Ammonium hydroxide
