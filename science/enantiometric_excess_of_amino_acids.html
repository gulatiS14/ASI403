Detection Steps for Enantiomeric Excess of L- or D-amino acids

(Naghashian-Haghighi et al., 2018)
  1. Sample Preparation

    1. Weigh 1.0 g of soil and transfer it to a sterile beaker.

    2. Add 10 mL of acetone to the beaker to hydrate the soil sample. Stir the mixture for 5 minutes to ensure even dispersion of particles.

  2. Reaction Phase

    • Add the NDA + L/D-GADE fluorescent probe to the filtered soil sample. Leave it for 5 minutes.

  3. Fluorescent Measurement

    1. Extract 1 mL of the solution and measure the fluorescence of the solution using a fluorescence spectrometer.

    2. Set the excitation wavelength to 300–700 nm and the emission wavelength to 300–700 nm to detect the specific peaks corresponding to L- or D-amino acids.

Reagent Required:

  1. Acetone
  2. NDA + L/D-GADE fluorescent probe
